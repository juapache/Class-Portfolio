# Resources

Supplementary readings, datasets, and reference materials for the course.

## Core Theoretical Texts

### Peace & Conflict Framework

- **Galtung, J.** (1969). "Violence, Peace, and Peace Research." *Journal of Peace Research*, 6(3), 167–191.
  - Foundational distinction between negative and positive peace
  - Introduces structural violence concept
  - Defines peace research methodology

- **Lederach, J. P.** (1997). *Building Peace: Sustainable Reconciliation in Divided Societies.* USIP Press.
  - Practical frameworks for peacebuilding
  - Emphasis on local ownership and grassroots approaches
  - Case studies from Central America

- **Ramsbotham, O., Miall, H., & Woodhouse, T.** (2011). *Contemporary Conflict Resolution.* Polity Press. (3rd ed.)
  - Comprehensive overview of resolution, management, and transformation approaches
  - Covers mediation, negotiation, and institutional mechanisms
  - Critical perspectives on peacekeeping and interventions

### Justice and Reconciliation

- **Sen, A.** (1999). *Development as Freedom.* Oxford University Press.
  - Connects development to human capabilities
  - Argues development is about expanding freedoms, not just economic growth
  - Relevant to positive peace as human flourishing

- **Tutu, D.** (1999). *No Future Without Forgiveness.* Doubleday.
  - Reflections on the South African Truth and Reconciliation Commission
  - Explores forgiveness, accountability, and healing
  - Personal and philosophical perspective on peace and justice

- **Minow, M.** (1998). *Between Vengeance and Forgiveness: Facing History after Genocide and Mass Violence.* Beacon Press.
  - Examines justice mechanisms in post-conflict societies
  - Compares truth commissions, prosecutions, and alternative approaches
  - Emphasizes deliberative democracy in addressing past harm

### Conflict Dynamics

- **Kaldor, M.** (2012). *New and Old Wars: Organized Violence in a Global Era.* Polity Press. (3rd ed.)
  - Analysis of contemporary conflict patterns
  - Discusses privatization of violence, diaspora involvement, identity politics
  - Relevant to understanding 21st-century conflicts

- **Collier, P.** (2007). *The Bottom Billion: Why the Poorest Countries Are Failing and What Can Be Done About It.* Oxford University Press.
  - Economic perspectives on conflict and development
  - Argues poverty, natural resources, and bad governance fuel conflict
  - Policy recommendations for peace and development

## Case Study Materials

### Recommended Case Studies (by region)

**Africa**
- Rwanda: Genocide, justice mechanisms, reconciliation
- South Africa: Apartheid, truth commission, democratic transition
- Somalia: State collapse, peacebuilding challenges
- Uganda: LRA conflict, transitional justice

**Asia**
- Cambodia: Khmer Rouge, peacebuilding and development
- Myanmar: Rohingya crisis, ongoing conflicts
- Philippines: Mindanao conflict, peace agreements

**Americas**
- Colombia: Drug trafficking, peace agreement (2016)
- El Salvador: Civil war aftermath, gang violence
- Northern Ireland: Historical conflict, peace process

**Middle East / Eastern Europe**
- Israeli-Palestinian: Ongoing conflict, negotiations
- Lebanon: Civil war, sectarian politics
- Ukraine: Russian invasion, sovereignty, justice

## Datasets & Tools

### Conflict Data

- [Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP)](https://ucdp.uu.se/) – Systematic documentation of armed conflicts
- [Correlates of War (COW)](https://correlatesofwar.org/) – Interstate and intrastate war data
- [Peace and Conflict Instability Ledger (PCIL)](https://www.systemicpeace.org/) – Stability assessments, conflict trends
